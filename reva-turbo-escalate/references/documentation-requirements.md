# Escalation Documentation Requirements

## Minimum Required Documentation

Every escalation note must contain all of the following sections. Incomplete documentation will be flagged.

### 1. Issue Summary (Required)

A clear, factual description of the issue in 2-3 sentences. Must answer:
- What is the issue?
- When was it identified?
- Who identified it?

**Example:** "PO-2026-0089 for Acme Corp has been delayed 18 days at the manufacturing stage. The China partner (Shenzhen MFG Co) reported a material shortage on 2026-03-15. The PM (Ray Yeh) identified the delay as exceeding the 2-week escalation threshold on 2026-03-25."

### 2. Impact Assessment (Required)

Quantify the impact across these dimensions:

| Dimension | Assessment |
|-----------|-----------|
| Customer Impact | How does this affect the customer? (delivery date, business operations, relationship) |
| Financial Impact | Dollar value at risk (order value, penalties, lost future business) |
| Timeline Impact | How many days delayed? Revised delivery date? |
| Relationship Impact | Risk to ongoing customer or partner relationship |
| Reputational Impact | Any risk to Rev A's reputation |

### 3. Root Cause (Required)

Known or suspected root cause of the issue. If root cause is unknown, state "Under investigation" and provide a timeline for root cause determination.

Categories:
- Manufacturing defect
- Material shortage/quality
- Shipping/logistics failure
- Communication breakdown
- Specification error
- Partner performance
- External factor (weather, customs, regulatory)
- Internal process gap

### 4. Actions Taken (Required)

Chronological list of actions the PM has already taken to address the issue before escalating:

| Date | Action | Result |
|------|--------|--------|
| {{DATE_1}} | {{ACTION_1}} | {{RESULT_1}} |
| {{DATE_2}} | {{ACTION_2}} | {{RESULT_2}} |

### 5. Recommendation (Required)

The PM's recommended course of action. Must be specific and actionable:
- What should be done?
- Who should do it?
- By when?
- What resources are needed?
- What is the expected outcome?

### 6. Timeline (Required)

| Milestone | Date |
|-----------|------|
| Issue identified | {{IDENTIFIED_DATE}} |
| Escalation initiated | {{ESCALATION_DATE}} |
| Response needed by | {{RESPONSE_DEADLINE}} |
| Resolution target | {{RESOLUTION_TARGET}} |

### 7. Supporting Documents (If Available)

List any supporting documents:
- NCR reports
- Inspection reports
- Email correspondence
- Photos/images
- Partner communications
- Customer communications

## Documentation Standards

1. **Factual tone.** State facts, not opinions. Avoid blame language.
2. **Precise dates.** Use specific dates, not "last week" or "recently."
3. **Dollar amounts.** Include specific dollar values for financial impact.
4. **Names and roles.** Identify people by name and role.
5. **Completeness.** All required sections must be populated. "N/A" is acceptable only for Supporting Documents.

## Resolution Documentation

When an escalation is resolved, document:

1. **Resolution description** — What was decided and done
2. **Resolved by** — Who made the final decision
3. **Resolution date** — When it was resolved
4. **Customer communication** — What was communicated to the customer
5. **Preventive action** — Steps to prevent recurrence
6. **Lessons learned** — Key takeaway for the team
