# Margin Guidelines

## Target Margins by Category

| Category | Target Margin | Minimum Margin | Approval for Below Min |
|----------|--------------|----------------|----------------------|
| Standard production parts | 28-35% | 22% | Senior PM |
| Complex/precision parts | 32-40% | 28% | Senior PM |
| Prototype/low volume (< 50 pcs) | 38-50% | 30% | Senior PM |
| Repeat orders (existing tooling) | 22-30% | 18% | PM |
| Strategic accounts (volume commitment) | 20-28% | 15% | Donovan Weber |
| Rush orders | Base margin + 15-30% premium | Base minimum | PM |
| Tooling/NRE | 15-25% | 10% | PM |
| Assembly/kitting | 30-40% | 25% | Senior PM |

## Margin Calculation

```
Margin % = (Quoted Price - COGS) / Quoted Price x 100
Quoted Price = COGS / (1 - Target Margin %)
```

Example: If COGS is $10.00 and target margin is 30%:
```
Quoted Price = $10.00 / (1 - 0.30) = $14.29
Margin = ($14.29 - $10.00) / $14.29 = 30%
```

## Margin Adjustment Factors

### Increase margin for:
- New customer (first order risk): +3-5%
- High complexity (score 4-5): +3-5%
- Tight tolerances requiring rework risk: +2-5%
- Exotic/volatile materials: +2-3%
- Export control overhead: +3-5%
- Customer with history of scope changes: +5-10%
- Short lead time / rush: +15-30%

### Decrease margin consideration for:
- High volume commitment (annual contract): -3-5%
- Repeat order (proven process): -2-3%
- Strategic account (growth potential): -3-8%
- Competitive situation (PM discretion): -2-5%
- Long-term relationship (3+ years): -2-3%

## Volume Break Points

| Volume Tier | Typical Discount from Base |
|------------|--------------------------|
| 1-49 pcs | Base price (no discount) |
| 50-249 pcs | 5-10% |
| 250-999 pcs | 10-18% |
| 1,000-4,999 pcs | 18-25% |
| 5,000+ pcs | 25-35% (negotiate individually) |

Note: Volume discounts come from manufacturing efficiency gains and material bulk pricing, not just margin reduction. Calculate actual COGS at each volume tier.

## Pricing Rules

1. **Never quote below minimum margin** without documented approval
2. **Show the PM all margins** in the internal buildup — they need to see the full picture
3. **Customer never sees margins** — only the quoted price, tooling, and shipping
4. **Round quoted prices** to appear natural — $14.25, not $14.2857
5. **Tooling margin is separate** — apply tooling margin to tooling line, not to unit price
6. **Shipping is pass-through plus handling** — add 5-10% handling on shipping costs
