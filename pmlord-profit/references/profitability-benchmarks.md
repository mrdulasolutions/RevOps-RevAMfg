## Minimum Acceptable Margin: 15%

Any order with actual gross margin below 15% must be escalated to Donovan Weber with:
- Root cause analysis (why did margin fall below minimum?)
- Was the issue in quoting (wrong estimate) or execution (cost overrun)?
- Is this a one-time issue or systemic?
- Recommended corrective action

## Red Line Margin: 0%

Any order that loses money (negative gross margin) requires:
- Immediate notification to Donovan Weber
- Full cost investigation
- Customer communication plan (if the customer needs to be informed of pricing changes)
- Corrective action plan for future orders

## Industry Benchmarks for Contract Manufacturing

These are external benchmarks for comparison. Rev A should aim to meet or exceed these.

| Metric | Industry Average | Top Quartile | Rev A Target |
|--------|-----------------|-------------|-------------|
| Gross margin (overall) | 20-25% | 30-35% | 25-30% |
| On-time delivery rate | 85-90% | 95%+ | 95%+ |
| COPQ (cost of poor quality) | 3-5% of revenue | <1% of revenue | <2% of revenue |
| Customer retention rate | 70-80% | 90%+ | 90%+ |
| Quote win rate | 20-30% | 40%+ | 35%+ |
| Average order margin erosion (quoted vs actual) | 3-5 points | <2 points | <3 points |

## Profitability Improvement Levers

When margin is below target, these are the levers available (in order of impact):

1. **Improve cost estimation accuracy** -- reduce variance between quoted and actual costs. Every 1% improvement in estimation accuracy = approximately 0.5% margin improvement.

2. **Negotiate partner pricing** -- for high-volume items or long-term commitments, negotiate better rates. Target: 5-10% reduction for annual volume commitments.

3. **Optimize shipping** -- consolidate shipments, plan for ocean vs air, avoid expedites. Air-to-ocean conversion saves 60-80% on shipping.

4. **Reduce quality costs** -- fewer NCRs, fewer re-inspections, fewer warranty claims. Every $1 spent on prevention saves $10 in failure costs.

5. **Increase prices** -- for customers below tier-appropriate margins, implement price increases at next quote opportunity. Target: bring all customers to at least minimum margin for their tier.

6. **Adjust product mix** -- focus sales effort on higher-margin processes and parts. Reduce effort on commoditized, low-margin work.

7. **Improve overhead efficiency** -- reduce PM time per order through automation (PMLORD skills), standardized processes, and better tools.
