# Voice Examples — PM Archetypes

This document shows four distinct PM communication archetypes with their voice profiles and sample emails. These illustrate the range of the voice system and serve as reference points during onboarding.

---

## Archetype 1: The Formal PM

### Profile Summary

| Dimension | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Tone | 2/10 |
| Formality | 2/10 |
| Technical depth | Deep |
| Sentence length | Long |
| Detail level | Thorough |
| Greeting | "Dear Mr./Ms. [Last]," |
| Signoff | "Respectfully," |
| Email length | Long |
| Bullet vs prose | Prose |
| Urgency language | Measured |
| Humor level | None |
| Report detail | Comprehensive |
| Quote cover | Formal letter |
| Partner reference | "our manufacturing partner" |

### Sample Email: Quote Submission

```
Dear Mr. Henderson,

Thank you for the opportunity to provide a quotation for the stainless
steel manifold assemblies referenced in your RFQ dated March 12, 2026.

I am pleased to present our pricing for the 1,000-unit production run.
The enclosed quotation reflects our competitive manufacturing capabilities
and includes all finishing operations as specified in your engineering
drawings (Rev D). The total unit price of $87.50 accounts for CNC
machining of the 316L stainless steel bodies, precision boring of the
four port holes to +/-0.002" tolerance, passivation per ASTM A967, and
final assembly with the O-ring seals you have specified.

Our projected lead time is eight weeks from receipt of your purchase order,
which includes three weeks for raw material procurement, four weeks for
machining and finishing, and one week for inspection and packaging. We have
confirmed material availability with our supplier and do not anticipate
any delays.

I would be pleased to arrange a call to discuss the quotation in detail
or to address any questions you may have regarding our manufacturing
approach. Please do not hesitate to contact me at your convenience.

Respectfully,
[PM Name]
Rev A Manufacturing
```

```
Hi Mark,

I've got the quote ready for those stainless steel manifold assemblies.
Here's the quick summary:

- 1,000 units: $87.50 each
- 316L stainless, machined and passivated per your Rev D specs

we're confident in the tolerances.

Take a look when you get a chance and let me know what you think. Happy
to jump on a call if it's easier to walk through the details together.

Best,
[PM Name]
```

## Archetype 4: The Technical PM

### Profile Summary

| Dimension | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Tone | 4/10 |
| Formality | 4/10 |
| Technical depth | Deep |
| Sentence length | Medium |
| Detail level | Thorough |
| Greeting | "Hi [First]," |
| Signoff | "Regards," |
| Email length | Long |
| Bullet vs prose | Mixed |
| Urgency language | Direct |
| Humor level | None |
| Report detail | Comprehensive |
| Quote cover | Detailed proposal |
| Partner reference | "our manufacturing partner in [City]" |

### Sample Email: Quote Submission

```
Hi Mark,

I've completed the quote for the 316L stainless steel manifold
assemblies per your RFQ and Rev D drawings. Attached is the formal
quotation; here's a technical summary.

Machining approach:
- Bodies: 4-axis CNC from 316L bar stock (ASTM A276)
- Port bores: +/-0.002" on all four, verified with bore gauge
- Surface finish: 32 Ra or better on sealing surfaces
- Passivation: ASTM A967, Citric 2 method

Pricing: $87.50/unit at 1,000 pieces. The cost driver is the tight bore
tolerance -- if you can open that to +/-0.005" on the two non-critical
ports, we could bring the price down to approximately $79/unit. I've
included both options in the attached quote.

Lead time breakdown:
- Material procurement: 3 weeks (316L bar stock in standard sizes)
- Machining: 3 weeks
- Passivation and inspection: 1 week
- Packaging and ship: 1 week
- Total: 8 weeks ARO

One note on the O-ring grooves: your drawing calls out a groove width of
0.142" +/-0.002". Our manufacturing partner in Shenzhen recommends
0.145" +/-0.003" for easier seal installation without affecting
performance. Let me know if you'd like to discuss this change.

Regards,
[PM Name]
```

## Archetype Comparison

| Dimension | Formal PM | Efficiency PM | Relationship PM | Technical PM |
|-----------|-----------|---------------|-----------------|--------------|
| Words in sample | ~210 | ~55 | ~130 | ~220 |
| Paragraphs | 4 | 2 | 4 | 5 |
| Bullets used | No | Yes | Yes | Yes |
| Technical detail | Deep | None | Light | Deep |
| Personal touch | None | None | Yes ("great talking") | None |
| Specs in body | Full | None | Key only | Full + alternatives |
| Next steps | Passive | Direct | Warm offer | Direct |
| Partner mention | Generic | None | "our team in..." | By location |

## Using These Archetypes

During onboarding, these archetypes can help a PM identify their style quickly:

> "Which of these four sample emails sounds most like something you would write?"

If the PM identifies with one archetype, use it as a starting point and refine from there. Most PMs will be a blend of two archetypes.
