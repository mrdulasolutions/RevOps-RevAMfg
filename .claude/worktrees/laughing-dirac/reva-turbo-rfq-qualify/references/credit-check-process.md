# Credit Check Process

## Credit Tiers

| Tier | Criteria | Terms | Approval |
|------|----------|-------|----------|
| **Tier 1: Established** | 12+ months history, no late payments, order history > $50K | Net 30 | PM approval |
| **Tier 2: Good** | 6-12 months history, 1 or fewer late payments, order history > $10K | Net 30 | PM approval |
| **Tier 3: New/Unproven** | < 6 months history or first order | Net 30 (pending credit check) or prepayment | Senior PM approval |
| **Tier 4: At Risk** | Multiple late payments, outstanding balance > 60 days, disputes | Prepayment or COD | Donovan Weber approval |
| **Tier 5: Blocked** | Delinquent account, legal action, unresolved disputes | No new orders | Donovan Weber approval to unblock |

## New Customer Credit Check

For new customers, the following steps are required before Net 30 terms are extended:

1. **Business verification** — Confirm the company exists (website, business registration, D&B number)
2. **Trade references** — Request 2-3 trade references from the customer
3. **Credit application** — Have the customer complete a credit application form
4. **D&B report** — Pull a Dun & Bradstreet report if the order value warrants it (> $25K)
5. **Initial order terms** — First order may require prepayment (50% deposit, 50% on shipment) or full prepayment

## Returning Customer Review

For returning customers, check:

1. **Payment history** — Review last 12 months of invoices
   - All paid within terms: CLEAR
   - 1-2 instances of 1-15 days late: CLEAR with note
   - Pattern of late payments (3+): FLAG
   - Any invoice > 60 days past due: RISK

2. **Outstanding balance** — Check for unpaid invoices
   - No outstanding: CLEAR
   - Outstanding but within terms: CLEAR
   - Outstanding and past due: FLAG or RISK depending on age

3. **Order value assessment** — Compare new order value to historical order size
   - Within 2x historical average: CLEAR
   - 2-5x historical average: FLAG for review
   - > 5x historical average: Requires additional credit review

## Credit Hold Triggers

Place a credit hold on new orders if:
- Any invoice is > 90 days past due
- Total outstanding balance exceeds $50,000
- Customer has bounced a payment
- Customer is unresponsive to payment inquiries for > 30 days

## Escalation

- Credit holds are communicated to the PM and Donovan Weber
- PM should contact the customer about outstanding payments before new work proceeds
- Donovan Weber has final authority on credit decisions and exceptions
