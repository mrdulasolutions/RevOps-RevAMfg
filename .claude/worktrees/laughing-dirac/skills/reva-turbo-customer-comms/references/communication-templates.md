# Communication Templates — Structure and Tone Guide

## Template 1: RFQ Acknowledgment

**Purpose:** Confirm receipt of an RFQ so the customer knows their request did not go into a black hole. Set expectations for next steps.

**Tone:** Standard. Professional and responsive. The goal is to make the customer feel like Rev A is already on it.

**Structure:**
1. Thank them for the RFQ
2. Confirm what was received (brief description, any files/drawings noted)
3. Set timeline for quote delivery
4. Ask any initial clarifying questions (if needed)
5. Provide PM contact for questions

**Length:** 3-4 short paragraphs

**Timing:** Send within 4 business hours of receiving the RFQ. Same day is the target.

**Key phrases:**
- "Thank you for your request for quote on..."
- "We have received your specifications and drawings and are reviewing them now."
- "You can expect our quote within [timeline]."
- "If you have any questions in the meantime, please do not hesitate to reach out."

## Template 2: Quote Submission

**Purpose:** Deliver the quote with all necessary details for the customer to make a decision. Make it easy to say yes.

**Tone:** Standard to Formal (depending on quote size). Confident and clear.

**Structure:**
1. Reference the original RFQ
2. Present the quote summary (price, quantity, lead time)
3. Note key terms (validity, payment terms, tooling)
4. Highlight any value-adds or differentiators
5. Clear instructions on how to place an order
6. PM availability for questions or adjustments

**Length:** 4-6 short paragraphs. Use a pricing table for clarity.

**Timing:** Per the timeline committed in the RFQ Acknowledgment. If earlier than promised, note it.

**Key phrases:**
- "Please find our quote for..."
- "This quote is valid for 30 days from the date above."
- "To place an order, please issue a PO referencing quote number [QT-XXXX]."
- "We are confident in our ability to deliver quality parts on schedule."
- "I am happy to discuss pricing, timeline, or specifications in more detail."

## Template 3: Order Confirmation

**Purpose:** Confirm receipt of the PO and set clear expectations for the order fulfillment process.

**Tone:** Standard. Confirmatory and reassuring. The customer just committed money; make them feel good about it.

**Structure:**
1. Confirm PO receipt and reference number
2. Summarize order details (part, qty, price, delivery)
3. Confirm payment terms
4. State when production begins
5. Set expectation for next status update
6. PM contact for questions

**Length:** 4-5 short paragraphs

**Timing:** Send within 24 hours of receiving the PO.

**Key phrases:**
- "Thank you for your order. We have received PO [number] and are ready to proceed."
- "Here is a summary of your order for your records."
- "Production is scheduled to begin on [date]."
- "You can expect your next status update by [date]."
- "We look forward to delivering quality parts on schedule."

## Template 4: Status Update

**Purpose:** Keep the customer informed on order progress. Proactive updates build trust and reduce inbound "where is my order" calls.

**Tone:** Standard (good news) or Urgent/Apologetic (delays or issues).

**Structure:**
1. Reference the order
2. State the current stage clearly
3. Provide detail on progress
4. Give updated timeline (especially if changed)
5. Note any issues or actions needed from customer
6. State when the next update will come

**Length:** 3-5 short paragraphs

**Timing:**
- Proactive updates: Weekly for active orders, or at major milestones
- Reactive updates: Same day when an issue is identified
- Customer-requested: Within 4 business hours

**Key phrases (good news):**
- "Your order is progressing on schedule."
- "Manufacturing is complete and parts are now in finishing."
- "We expect to ship on [date] as planned."

**Key phrases (delay or issue):**
- "I want to give you an update on your order, including a timeline adjustment."
- "We have identified [issue] and are taking the following steps to resolve it."
- "The revised estimated delivery is [date]. We apologize for the inconvenience."
- See `references/escalation-language.md` for detailed guidance.

## Template 5: Shipment Notification

**Purpose:** Notify the customer that their order has shipped with all tracking and delivery details.

**Tone:** Standard. This is good news — parts are on the way.

**Structure:**
1. Announce the shipment
2. Provide tracking information (carrier, tracking number, URL)
3. State estimated delivery date
4. Summarize what was shipped (packing list)
5. Note any receiving or delivery instructions
6. Mention invoice details if applicable
7. Thank them and invite feedback

**Length:** 4-5 short paragraphs

**Timing:** Same day as shipment. Within 2 hours of carrier pickup if possible.

**Key phrases:**
- "Your order has shipped and is on its way."
- "You can track your shipment using the following information."
- "Estimated delivery: [date]."
- "Please inspect upon receipt and let us know if you have any questions."
- "Thank you for your business."
