# Tooling Cost Reference

## Injection Mold Tooling (China Partner)

| Mold Type | Small (< 6" x 6") | Medium (6"-12") | Large (12"-24") |
|-----------|-------------------|-----------------|-----------------|
| Single cavity, simple | $2,000-5,000 | $5,000-12,000 | $12,000-25,000 |
| Single cavity, complex | $5,000-12,000 | $12,000-25,000 | $25,000-50,000 |
| Multi-cavity (2-4), simple | $5,000-15,000 | $15,000-30,000 | $30,000-60,000 |
| Multi-cavity (4-8), medium | $12,000-30,000 | $30,000-60,000 | $60,000-120,000 |
| Multi-cavity (8+), complex | $25,000-60,000 | $60,000-120,000 | $120,000-250,000 |
| Family mold (multiple parts) | $8,000-20,000 | $20,000-45,000 | $45,000-100,000 |

### Mold Complexity Factors
- Hot runner system: +$3,000-15,000 depending on drops
- Side actions/slides: +$1,500-5,000 per action
- Lifters: +$1,000-3,000 per lifter
- Unscrewing mechanism: +$3,000-10,000
- Texture/surface treatment: +$1,000-5,000
- Mold flow analysis: +$500-2,000

### Mold Steel Grades
| Grade | Application | Cost Impact |
|-------|------------|------------|
| P20 | Low volume (< 100K shots) | Base price |
| H13 | Medium volume (100K-500K) | +15-25% |
| S136 / NAK80 | High volume (500K+), optical | +25-40% |
| Hardened steel | Very high volume (1M+) | +30-50% |

### Mold Ownership
- Standard: Customer owns the mold, stored at China partner facility
- Rev A retains production rights — mold cannot be transferred without agreement
- Mold maintenance is included for the first 100K shots; after that, quoted separately

## Tooling Quoting Rules

1. **Always quote tooling separately** as an NRE line item
2. **Apply 15-25% margin** on tooling costs from China partner
3. **Include mold flow analysis** if the customer requests it or if the part is complex
4. **Note mold ownership terms** on every injection mold quote
5. **First article samples** (T1) are typically included in tooling cost; production-quality samples (T2+) may be additional
6. **Tooling modifications** after T1 approval are quoted separately

## Amortization Option

If the customer requests tooling cost amortized into unit price:

```
Amortized cost per unit = Tooling cost / Expected lifetime volume
Adjusted unit price = Standard unit price + Amortized cost per unit
```

Note: Amortization carries risk if the customer does not reach expected volume. PM should assess likelihood before agreeing to amortize. Minimum order commitment may be required.
