# Anti-Dumping / Countervailing Duty Orders — China

AD/CVD duties are imposed when the U.S. International Trade Commission (ITC) determines that Chinese producers are selling goods in the U.S. at less than fair value (dumping) or benefiting from government subsidies (countervailing).

**CRITICAL:** AD/CVD rates can be 50-200%+ on top of MFN and Section 301 rates. Misclassifying an item subject to AD/CVD is one of the most expensive compliance errors.

## How to Check If Your Product Is In Scope

1. **Product description:** Does the item match the scope language of an AD/CVD order?
2. **HTS code:** Is the HTS code listed in the order's scope?
3. **Material:** Is it the specific material covered (e.g., aluminum extrusion vs cast)?
4. **Manufacturing process:** Some orders are process-specific (e.g., extrusion vs machining)
5. **CBP scope rulings:** Check for scope determinations at enforcement.trade.gov

## What to Do If AD/CVD May Apply

1. **HOLD the entry.** Do not clear customs until AD/CVD status is confirmed.
2. **Check the scope.** Read the actual scope language, not just the product name.
3. **Request a scope ruling** from Commerce if ambiguous.
4. **Consult a customs broker** with AD/CVD experience.
5. **Escalate in PMLORD** via pmlord-escalate.
6. **Factor into quoting** — if Rev A regularly imports AD/CVD-subject goods, the duty must be in the cost estimate.

## Rates Change

AD/CVD rates are recalculated annually through administrative reviews. The rates above are approximate. Always verify current rates at:
- enforcement.trade.gov (DOC Enforcement & Compliance)
- USITC AD/CVD database
- TradeInsights.ai (real-time rates)
