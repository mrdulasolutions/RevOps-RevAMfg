# Escalation Language Guide

Language patterns for communicating issues, delays, and bad news to customers. The goal is to be honest, accountable, and solution-focused.

## General Principles

1. **Lead with the facts.** Do not bury bad news. State it clearly in the first or second sentence.
2. **Own it.** Do not blame manufacturing partners, shipping carriers, or other departments. Rev A is one entity to the customer.
3. **Provide the solution or plan.** Never communicate a problem without a plan to fix it.
4. **Give a revised timeline.** If the original timeline is affected, provide a new one.
5. **Do not over-apologize.** One sincere apology is more effective than three. Move quickly to the solution.

## Delay Communication

### Minor Delay (1-5 business days)

```
I want to provide an update on your order [reference].

We are experiencing a slight delay in [manufacturing/finishing/shipping]
due to [brief, honest reason]. Your revised estimated delivery is [new date],
which is [X days] beyond our original commitment.

We are monitoring this closely and will provide another update by [date]
or sooner if the situation changes.

I apologize for the inconvenience. Please let me know if this revised
timeline creates any issues on your end.
```

### Significant Delay (1-2 weeks)

```
I need to let you know about a delay on your order [reference].

[Brief explanation of the issue]. As a result, our estimated delivery
has moved from [original date] to [new date].

Here is what we are doing to minimize the impact:
- [Action 1]
- [Action 2]
- [Action 3 — if applicable]

I understand this may affect your plans, and I want to discuss options
with you. Would you be available for a quick call [suggested time]?

I apologize for this delay and am committed to getting your parts to you
as quickly as possible.
```

### Major Delay (2+ weeks)

```
I am reaching out about a significant development on your order [reference]
that I want to discuss with you directly.

[Clear explanation of the issue and its impact on delivery]. Our revised
delivery estimate is [new date].

I want to be transparent about the situation and work with you on the
best path forward. Options include:
- [Option 1 — e.g., accept revised timeline]
- [Option 2 — e.g., partial shipment of completed items]
- [Option 3 — e.g., air freight to compress timeline]

I would like to schedule a call at your earliest convenience to discuss.
Please let me know what works for you.

I sincerely apologize for this disruption. Rev A takes our delivery
commitments seriously, and we are fully focused on resolving this.
```

## Quality Issue Communication

### Issue Found During Rev A Inspection (before shipment)

```
During our quality inspection of your order [reference], we identified
[brief description of issue] on [number] of [total] parts.

We are [taking the following corrective action / remanufacturing the
affected parts / working with our manufacturing team to resolve this].
This will add approximately [X days/weeks] to the delivery timeline.

The good news is that we caught this before shipment, and the replacement
parts will meet all of your specifications. Your revised delivery
estimate is [new date].

I will provide an update as soon as the replacement parts clear inspection.
```

### Issue Reported by Customer (after delivery)

```
Thank you for bringing this to our attention. I am sorry to hear about
the issue with [description].

I would like to understand the problem fully so we can resolve it quickly.
Could you please provide:
- [Specific question about the issue]
- [Request for photos or measurements if applicable]
- [Quantity affected]

In the meantime, I am [initiating our corrective action process /
preparing replacement parts / coordinating with our quality team].

We take quality seriously at Rev A, and we will make this right.
```

## Pricing / Cost Issue Communication

### Price Increase on Repeat Order

```
As we prepare your quote for the next run of [part], I want to let you
know about a pricing adjustment.

Due to [material cost increases / tooling wear requiring maintenance /
process changes], the unit price has moved from $[old] to $[new],
representing a [X]% change.

I have worked to minimize this adjustment and can discuss further if
the new pricing is a concern. We value our ongoing partnership and want
to find a solution that works for both of us.
```

## Do Not Say

| Instead of... | Say... |
|--------------|--------|
| "Our factory in China messed up" | "We encountered a manufacturing issue" |
| "It is not our fault" | "Here is what happened and what we are doing" |
| "I do not know when" | "Our current estimate is [date], and I will update you by [date]" |
| "Sorry for any inconvenience" | "I apologize for this delay/issue" (be specific) |
| "You should have told us sooner" | "Going forward, please flag [thing] early so we can address it" |
| "We will try to fix it" | "Here is our plan to resolve this" |
| "That is not covered" | "Let me explain what is included and discuss options for [additional need]" |
| "Per my last email" | [Just restate the information directly] |
